If you're a content creator in 2026, you're already behind if you're not using AI. The question isn't *whether* to use AI tools — it's *which ones* to combine into a stack that actually works.

We've tested dozens of combinations. Here's the stack that saves the most time without sacrificing quality.

## The Core Stack

Every content creator needs four layers:

1. **Writing & Ideation** — generating drafts, outlines, and repurposing content
2. **Visual Creation** — images, thumbnails, social graphics
3. **Video & Audio** — editing, transcription, voiceover
4. **Publishing & Distribution** — scheduling, analytics, management

## Layer 1: Writing & Ideation

<Callout type="tip">
The best writing AI isn't the one that writes *for* you — it's the one that writes *with* you. Use AI for first drafts and outlines, then add your voice.
</Callout>

For long-form content, **Claude** is our top pick. Its extended context window means you can paste an entire article draft and get meaningful structural feedback. For quick social copy and brainstorming, **ChatGPT** remains the fastest.

**Our recommendation:** Use both. Claude for deep work, ChatGPT for quick iterations.

## Layer 2: Visual Creation

**Midjourney** leads for custom illustrations and hero images. **Canva** fills the gap for social templates, thumbnails, and branded graphics. Together they cover 95% of a creator's visual needs.

The key insight: don't try to replace your design sense with AI. Use AI to *accelerate* it.

## Layer 3: Video & Audio

**Descript** has become the Swiss Army knife of video editing. Edit video by editing text. Remove filler words in one click. Generate captions automatically.

For creators who need more control, pair Descript with a traditional editor. For everyone else, it's the entire video pipeline.

## Layer 4: Publishing & Management

**Notion** serves as the content hub — editorial calendar, idea database, SOPs, and collaboration space. It doesn't generate content, but it makes sure nothing falls through the cracks.

## What to Skip

Not every trending AI tool deserves a spot in your stack:

- **AI scheduling tools** — Most social platforms have built-in scheduling now. Don't pay $30/month for what's free.
- **AI SEO writers** — They produce generic content that Google increasingly penalizes. Write with AI assistance, not AI automation.
- **All-in-one suites** — Tools that claim to do everything usually do nothing well. Specialized tools, loosely coupled, always win.
